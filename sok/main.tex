\section{Benchmark harness}
\label{app:bench}

The first-party measurements in row C1 of \S\ref{sec:analysis} are
produced by the harness at \texttt{benchmarks/c1/} in this paper's
source tree. The C1 deployment under measurement consists of
\emph{five Soroban contracts}, one policy per contract, with each
contract holding two relations (membership and update) compiled at
three circuit-size tiers; all thirty verifying keys derive from a
single SRS --- the Ethereum Foundation KZG Summoning
Ceremony~\cite{eth-kzg-ceremony} --- whose provenance is hash-chained
through the build (\S\ref{sec:cfg-c1}). The harness measures each
contract independently; the §4 tables aggregate per-contract numbers
into the C1 row, and expose per-contract sub-rows in the cost table
where the variation across contracts is load-relevant.
\texttt{methodology.md} documents the measurement contract (what is
and is not timed; hardware and software pinning; the per-contract
\texttt{results.json} schema; the SRS-pinning hash chain).
\texttt{run.sh} iterates over the five contracts, executes their
provers and a Soroban verify call, and writes \texttt{results.json},
which the table cells in Tables~\ref{tab:cost}--\ref{tab:pq} consume
via generated \texttt{.tex} snippets. Per-call sampling
instrumentation within a single proof is deferred pending a
dedicated benchmark binary; the current harness reports a coarse
\texttt{wall\_clock\_ms\_total} per contract.
